Inside most organizations, strategy is rarely the problem. Leaders define ambitious goals, build roadmaps, and set priorities. But somewhere between the leadership team and the daily work of employees, the energy behind those ideas fades.

The challenge inside modern companies

Most employees want to care deeply about their work. They want to understand the mission of the organization and see how their efforts contribute to something meaningful.

But as organizations grow, complexity grows with them. Teams specialize. Communication fragments. Priorities multiply.

And slowly the connection between the strategy at the top and the work across the organization becomes harder to see. Not because people don't care. But because the story of the company gets lost in the noise.

That’s where activation matters.

The moment when everything clicks

During Ben’s keynote, something shifts in the room. Employees begin to see the larger story of the company again. Leaders hear the language their teams use to talk about motivation and alignment.

There is more laughter. More honesty. More curiosity.

And suddenly the room feels different. Teams remember that they are not just completing tasks. They are building something together.

Meet Ben Hawes

Ben Hawes has spent more than a decade helping organizations activate their people.

His career spans leadership and engagement roles at companies like ClassPass, LifeLabs Learning, and Rocket Money where he built learning programs, leadership development initiatives, and culture systems designed to connect employees to the mission of the organizations they serve.

Alongside his work in learning and development, Ben runs a corporate events company focused on engagement experiences for teams. Through that work he has partnered with organizations including Uber, Meta, Framebridge, Macy’s, Brooklyn Museum, HelloFresh, WeWork, and Faire.
