Hi! I’m Ben. I spend a lot of time inside companies helping teams reconnect to something that leaders care deeply about but sometimes struggle to unlock.

Energy.

Most organizations do not actually have a strategy problem. The strategy is thoughtful. The goals are clear. The leadership team has worked incredibly hard to define the direction.

But somewhere between the strategy deck and the day to day work, the energy starts to drift a little.

Let’s be honest about something

Inside almost every organization there is a moment when leadership looks around and thinks… we have a great strategy. So why does it feel like we are pushing a little harder than we should have to?

It is not because people do not care.

Most employees genuinely want to do great work. They want to contribute. They want to feel proud of what they are building together.

But work moves fast. Priorities shift. Slack messages multiply. Calendars fill up. And slowly, without anyone noticing, the connection to the bigger mission gets a little fuzzy.

That is where I come in.

I help teams reconnect to the mission, remember why their work matters, and bring fresh energy back into the room.

The Activation Question

During the keynote I ask everyone in the room a simple question.

On a scale of 1 to 10… how activated are you in your work right now?

Not how busy you are. Not how many meetings you have this week.

But how energized you actually feel about the work you are doing.

That one question opens the door to a conversation that teams rarely get to have.

And once that conversation starts, something really interesting happens in the room.

The Activation Framework

After that reflection, we walk through a simple model that helps teams reconnect to the mission and build momentum again.

The idea is simple. When teams have clarity, honest communication, and a moment to recommit to the work ahead, something shifts.

You can literally feel the energy change in the room.

Clarity

People reconnect their daily work to the bigger mission and understand how their role contributes to the direction of the company.

Communication

Teams have honest conversations about motivation, priorities, and where energy might be slipping.

Commitment

Each person reflects on how they want to show up moving forward and what it means to bring their best energy to the work.
